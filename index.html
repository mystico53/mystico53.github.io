Products I've built

All of the following products are my creations. I envisioned the initial concept, pitched the idea to generate funding, hired teams, executed the development, tested and iterated on prototypes and MVPs. All of these products grow between 1.4x and 1.7x YoY - just by word of mouth.

Senaryon

Senaryon is a complex, multi-layered web application that I created to scale the company's face-to-face business model. It specifically targets schools and universities, enabling teachers, professors, and large institutions to autonomously facilitate complex workshops.

After developing a highly succesful MVP within three months, I incrementally added core features to increase its ease of use, such as a real-time task system, a moderators area, a mobile friendly design system and interactive elements that improve immersion.

Senaryon is a first-of-its-kind simulation engine of societal conflicts, which drew attention from educational researchers. They encouraged me to publish four peer-reviewed articles. I represented the product at 30 conferences and playtested face-to-face with 1000 students.

50000+ users available in nine languages used by 200 universities used by 2000 schools

Junait

Designed to look and function like a social network, Junait (pronounced “Unite) is an interactive platform teaching digital literacy and internet safety to elementary school kids (age 8 - 12) in German-speaking regions. It has been super rewarding to work with young kids, seeing their digital skills and confidence grow.

Designed to look and function like a social network, Junait (pronounced “Unite) is an interactive platform teaching digital literacy and internet safety to elementary school kids (age 8 - 12) in German-speaking regions. It has been super rewarding to work with young kids, seeing their digital skills and confidence grow.

I partnered with Germany’s renown childbook author Thomas Feibel to create a compelling narrative. This narrative connects the technical features to the learning objectives. The network acts like a haunted house, infested by the evil Dr. Virus and filled with staged dangers like bots and viruses, designed to ensnare and deceive the students. They assist the cool hacker Condor, to turn the network into a safe place.

Unionslabor

Unionslabor, (translated as EU-Laboratory) is a serious game I created for high-school students. It focuses on the art of governance while teaching conflict resolution and perspective taking skills.

With a size of only three Megabytes, this mobile web-application allows students and teachers in any classroom to experience the beneficial effects of modern technology. The game format reflects our own use of technology, talking to eachother, while interacting digitally with our phones.

My initial concept succesfully secured funding from two major German foundations. I designed all game mechanics, and created a team of game designers, EU-Policy Experts and Instructional Designers. A narrative with a wink ties policy domain knowledge to engaging interactions. This offers a welcomed package to teachers that want to connect technology, interaction and civic education.
