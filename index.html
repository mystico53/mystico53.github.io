Senaryon

As the architect and lead in developing planpolitik's online education arm, I directed the creation of Senaryon, a unique game engine that successfully digitized traditional learning methods, significantly expanding our reach to 50,000 students in schools and universities. I managed a $2 million budget and a diverse team of 12 talents, swiftly building a working prototype in three months, and driving the product to market. This was achieved through agile development and strategic collaborations, including partnering with universities for UX and ed-tech research (1, 2, 3, 4), introducing innovative license-based payment models, and implementing company-wide collaboration processes, which were instrumental in navigating and thriving during the pandemic.

Junait

In developing Junait, a digital literacy tool for elementary schools, I led the project from concept to reality, securing $400,000 in governmental funding. This platform, designed like a social network, is integrated into daily classroom activities in German-speaking countries, educating students on online dangers such as stranger interaction, data theft, and virus threats. Collaborating with digital literacy experts and renowned author Thomas Feibel, I played a pivotal role in shaping Junait's engaging narrative and functionality. The platform, tested extensively with 500 students, is now an award-winning tool recognized for enhancing online security awareness, receiving notable media coverage including in Die Zeit & by the Federal Association of Consumer Centers.

EU-Laboratory

In my role developing "EU-Laboratory," known as "Unionslabor" in German, an educational game that demystifies European Union politics for high school students, I successfully led the project from its conceptualization, securing funding from Bertelsmann and Heinz-Nixdorf Foundation, to its widespread daily use in classrooms. This game, embraced by teachers and students alike for its engaging approach, was brought to life by a cross-functional team I assembled, including a game designer and instructional designer. Our "Bring Your Own Device" strategy facilitated easy integration into educational settings. Beyond development, I crafted the marketing website and produced the game trailer (see above), contributing significantly to the game's popularity and its recognition through media coverage and award nominations.
