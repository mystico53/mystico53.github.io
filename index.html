Senaryon

A groundbreaking web application I developed, revolutionizing the face-to-face educational model for schools and universities. This platform allows educators to easily create and run complex workshops, enhancing learning experiences. Key achievements include a successful MVP launch within three months, continuous feature enhancements like real-time tasks, a moderator area, and mobile responsiveness. Recognized as a unique societal conflict simulation engine, Senaryon has gained acclaim in academic circles, leading to four peer-reviewed publications (1, 2, 3, 4) and presentations at 30 conferences. It has been playtested with over 1000 students, demonstrating its practical impact in education.

Junait

Junait, styled as a social network, is an innovative digital literacy and internet safety platform for 8-12-year-olds in German-speaking areas. Collaborating with celebrated author Thomas Feibel Thomas Feibel, we crafted a narrative where students navigate a 'haunted' network, outwitting the villainous Dr. Virus. This engaging, interactive environment, complete with bots and viruses, not only teaches but also captivates young users. Despite initial skepticism, the platform's success is evident in the vibrant, magical atmosphere it creates in classrooms and its unique appeal as an educational multiplayer experience, making it a favorite among teachers.

25000+ completed 90 min sessions 10 months development time used by 1500 schools

EU-Laboratory

EU-Laboratory, known as "Unionslabor" in German, is a serious game I developed for high-school students. It innovatively teaches governance, conflict resolution, and perspective-taking skills. My original concept garnered funding from two major German foundations, leading to the development of this game with a team of designers, EU policy experts, and instructional designers. The game cleverly combines policy knowledge with engaging interactions, offering an appealing educational tool for teachers integrating technology and civic education. Remarkably compact at just three megabytes, this mobile web-app is accessible in any classroom, mirroring real-life digital interaction and underscoring the positive impact of technology in education.
