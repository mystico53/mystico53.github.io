What if someone is always looking for the next job for you?

August 2025

Why did I start working on job recommendations? First of all, I was unemployed but c'mon, let's not overcomplicate it: Even though jobsearch is a complex process, the beginning is fairly easy: Reading the actual job description and deciding if it fits. Simple! Can you do that? Let's do a test:

Imagine you're reading a job description, now how much intelligence do you use when answering the following two questions?

  • What are the requirements of the job?
  • Can I deliver that?

I bet you just thought: "I can answer these questions without much effort". And you're right! The real pain as a job searcher is not reading one but dozens of job descriptions, every single day.

Learning 1: There are no incentives to write good job descriptions

Remember: You need to write your resume in a certain format, so that a recruiter can understand it within 3 seconds -> Company, Title, Duration, Bulletpoints ("Accomplished cool X as measured by metric Y, by doing sth Z).

This clear universal format does not exist for writing job descriptions. This makes it much harder to read a lot of job descriptions than to read a lot of resumes. Today's job descriptions are a funnel of shame that could care less how much time job applicants spend on understanding what their job is about.

Common Job Description Issues:
  • Full of generic industry lingo
  • Written by LLMs
  • Less than one sentence on what the company does
  • Unclear specifications and responsibilities
  • Unclear team environment and growth opportunities

How I want job descriptions to look like, so that I can read them in 7 seconds:

  • What the company does (All in one sentence: Context, Industry, Growthphase)
  • My main responsibilities (Specific tasks, no "cross-functional communication" bs)
  • Why I might be NOT considered (Missing domain expertise, certain certificate required..)

Instead of whining about poor job descriptions: Let's ask LLMs to help us, cause that's where they SHINE:
LLMs understand meaning, not just keywords. They can deduct that "managing a development" counts as "leadership experience" and understand that "tracking KPIs" counts as "data-anaylsis".

How I planned to build an Agent that revolutionizes job recommendations
  1. Read job postings
  2. Match with resume and personal preferences
  3. Receive only the jobs that are worthy for your eyes

So there you go, I started to develop a tool that automates that. Why? I believe the idea of creating truly trustworthy job recommendations is powerful. Active job seekers save at least 30 mins per day (or 15+ hrs a month), passive job seekers are brought into the game, because they usually don't spend any time on job search. Might this be worth $6.99 if I can deliver on "actually useful job recommendations"? I decided to take the risk.
