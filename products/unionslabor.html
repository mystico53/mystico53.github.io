Unionslabor Product Page

Introduction

Embark on a journey through the intricate politics of the European Union with "Unionslabor," a compelling educational game that I have had the honor of developing. Designed to engage high school students in Germany, Unionslabor offers a hands-on experience of supranational governance, challenging players to make pivotal decisions as heads of government using their smartphones.

Funding and Conceptualization

The inception of Unionslabor was marked by a successful pitch that secured funding from prestigious institutions like the Bertelsmann and Heinz-Nixdorf Foundation. This financial backing was a vote of confidence in the game's potential to educate and inspire.

Development and Design

As the creative force behind Unionslabor, I crafted the Game Design Document and laid out the wireframes for the game mechanics. The 'Bring your own device' strategy was a deliberate choice to streamline the game's facilitation in classrooms, making the EU's complex decision-making processes accessible to all.

Team Assembly and Leadership

To bring Unionslabor to life, I assembled a dynamic team comprising a game designer, an author, an illustrator, two scientists, and an instructional designer. Together, we translated the intricate workings of the EU into an engaging game format, directed by my hands-on leadership.

Testing and Marketing

The game was refined through feedback workshops with 500 students, ensuring that the final product resonated with its target audience. My role extended to marketing efforts, where I created persuasive copy for the website and produced an attention-grabbing game trailer.

Daily Integration and Accolades

Unionslabor has since become a staple in German-speaking classrooms, used daily to educate the next generation of EU citizens. Its impact has been recognized through shortlistings for both European and national awards.

Media Recognition

The significance of Unionslabor has not gone unnoticed, with features in major publications like Bild-Zeitung and Familie & Co, as well as coverage in influential blogs, highlighting its role in shaping how students perceive and understand the European Union.

Call to Action

Unionslabor is more than a game; it's an educational tool that brings the European Union to life. I encourage educators and students alike to explore the power and potential of this platform. For more information, visit www.unionslabor.de, and join us in navigating the fascinating realm of EU politics.
